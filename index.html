CENTRE FOR MANUSCRIPT AND TEXT CULTURES

The Centre for Manuscript and Text Cultures is an academic hub for inter-disciplinary activities related to the study of pre-modern manuscript and epigraphic traditions from different cultures. The Centre has been established by The Queen’s College, Oxford, but is proactively engaged in collaborative activities involving scholars from other institutions around the world.

A new manuscript discovered in South China

A successful fieldwork expedition conducted by Dr. Baker as part of his research project at CMTC has let him to a discovery of a seventeenth-century manuscript preserved in a local shrine in the city of Chaozhou in Guangdong province. The manuscript contains ancient longevity recipes and is thought to provide key evidence concerning the development of local Daoist cults in this poorly studied region.

MANUSCRIPT AND TEXT CULTURES: Latest Issue

The inaugural issue of Manuscript and Text Cultures explores the issue of transposition and monumentality in a range of pre-modern manuscript and epigraphic traditions. Driving on a broad range of case studies from ancient Egypt, Mesopotamia, Levant, China, as well as medieval Europe, the issue offers a fresh contribution to the ongoing debates on the monumentality as a cross-cultural phenomenon. In addition, it provides valuable insights into the transformations of texts and textual messages as they travel between media, becoming transposed in time and space.
